## About the Hackathon

**Theme:** An Eco Revolution – Empowering Youth for Green Economies  
**Duration:** October 21 – November 28, 2025  
**Award Ceremony:** November 28, 2025  
**Mode:** Hybrid – YouTube, Discord, GitHub  
**Organized by:** Armely + AIESEC in Moi University

The Eco Revolution Hackathon is a three-week innovation journey helping students explore how AI, data, and automation can drive sustainability.

Participants will gain hands-on experience with:

- Microsoft Technologies  
- Copilot Studio  
- Microsoft Fabric  
- Azure AI Foundry  

through guided training, mentorship, and collaborative building.

## Focus Pillar: Green Innovation & Solutions

Aligned with Youth Month 2025’s Green Innovation and Solutions, the hackathon explores how technology can support circular economies, sustainability, and environmental protection.

### Key Tracks

---

### 1. Climate Tech & Agri-Tech
Build solutions around renewable energy, sustainable agriculture, and environmental monitoring.

**Example ideas:**
- Smart irrigation  
- AI for crop health  
- Carbon-tracking dashboards  

---

### 2. Green Entrepreneurship & Circular Economy
Solutions that transform waste into opportunity and promote responsible production.

**Example ideas:**
- Recycling marketplaces  
- Green finance tools  
- Reuse-and-repair systems  

---

### 3. Carbon Awareness & Offsetting
Apps and dashboards that measure, visualize, and reduce carbon footprints.

**Example ideas:**
- Gamified carbon tracking  
- Tree-planting networks  
- Sustainability challenges

## Training Experience

### Week 1 – Copilot Studio (Led by Edgar Ochieng’)
**Focus:** Intelligent agents and workflow automation.  
Learn how to design conversational AI experiences that drive sustainability impact.

**Mini Project:**  
Build a chatbot that educates users on personal carbon reduction tips.

---

### Week 2 – Microsoft Fabric (Led by Sammy Chesire & Martin Wambui)
**Focus:** Data storytelling for sustainability.  
Learn ingestion, transformation, visualization, and dashboarding using Microsoft Fabric.

**Mini Project:**  
Dashboard showing the relationship between energy consumption and renewable investment.

---

### Week 3 – Azure AI Foundry (Led by Malvin Owuor)
**Focus:** Bringing AI ideas to life.  
Learn model creation, evaluation, deployment, and building predictive sustainability systems.

**Mini Project:**  
Build a predictive model that forecasts renewable energy potential based on weather data.

## The Challenge

From Day 1, you’ll begin shaping a real solution you can prototype during the three weeks.

You may build solo or in teams of up to four.  
Use any technology stack — as long as your solution aligns with **Green Innovation**.

We are not looking for perfect.  
We are looking for **purposeful**, well-thought-out solutions.

---

## Project Guidelines

### Teams
- Solo or teams of up to four.

### Required Deliverables (Due Tue, November 11 at Midnight)
- **Source Code** (GitHub Repo)  
- **5-Minute Video Pitch**  
- **Documentation (1–2 pages)** describing:
  - problem  
  - solution  
  - technology stack  
  - impact  

### Focus On
- Originality & creativity  
- Real-world application  
- Simplicity & clarity  
- Impact & scalability
